> What if the land had its own language?<br/>
> No alphabet but steady drone<br/>
> of grasslands, groan of mountains,<br/>
> drought-fire’s scream—a drawn-out cry,<br/>
> hiss of rain, simmer of seeds<br/>
> stirring restless in the soil<br/>
> pure presence and process<br/>
> breaking into the place<br/>
> made new by cataclysm.
> <cite><i>[National Forest](https://poems.com/poem/national-forest)</i> by Alison Hawthorne Deming, excerpt</cite>
