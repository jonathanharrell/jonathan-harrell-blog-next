> The rain consumed the people<br/>
> and left them with empty streets, mud-stained<br/>
> walls, and roads black with flooding.<br/>
> There are no ghosts here. They drained away<br/>
> with the ebbing waters. What is left<br/>
> is the soft wind off the river and the hum<br/>
> that comes with the loss of all memory.
> <cite><i>[After the Deluge](https://poems.com/poem/after-the-deluge)</i> by Kwame Dawes, excerpt</cite>
